# SDG Alignment — AirSense

## Primary: SDG 11 — Sustainable Cities and Communities

**Target 11.6:** "By 2030, reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including
by paying special attention to air quality and municipal and other waste management."

How AirSense contributes:
- Directly helps citizens understand and respond to urban air pollution levels
- Encourages behavior change (route/timing adjustments, indoor activity on bad-AQI days) that
  reduces personal exposure and, in aggregate, can shift city-wide commuting/activity patterns
- Provides a free, scalable tool that could integrate into municipal civic-tech platforms

## Secondary: SDG 3 — Good Health and Well-being

**Target 3.9:** "By 2030, substantially reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous
chemicals and air, water and soil pollution and contamination."

How AirSense contributes:
- Delivers personalized health-risk guidance based on individual vulnerability factors (age,
  pre-existing conditions)
- Specifically supports the WHO's identified high-risk groups: children, elderly, and people with
  asthma, heart conditions, or who are pregnant
- Encourages preventive action (masks, timing, indoor alternatives) rather than reactive healthcare

## Why these two SDGs together

Air quality sits at the intersection of urban environmental management (SDG 11) and individual
health outcomes (SDG 3). AirSense's core value is translating city-level environmental data into
individual-level health decisions — making it a natural bridge between these two goals.
